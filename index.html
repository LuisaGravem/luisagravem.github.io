Claim

The black box does not contain a main tube that has two holes (that connect to another tube) and an output tube connected to the second tube

Evidence

Initially, the data we collected from the box showed that different amounts of water came out of the box with no regard to the amount of water inputted.

My group's approach was to create a water reserve with two points the water could empty out

depending on the amount of water in the box. Our results ended up being the opposite of the initial results.

For example, when we input 50 mL, 25 mL consistently comes out

Further, each output was less than the input.

Reasoning One

Models are (usually) scaled-down replicas of something else. They are useful because they allow someone to adjust an existing

idea/object without destroying the original. Limitations can include materials, sturdiness, and size. Strengths include how easy it is to

make many and how easy it is to adjust. The consistency of my group's results is the opposite of what we were aiming for, because

the original data was extremely inconsistent.

Reasoning Two

The claim is correct because our data implies that there is some consistency to our results, which is the opposite of the original boxâ€™s

data. Our data is convincing because we did five trials for every quantity of water.

Reasoning Three

If the proposed model was correct, our data would have varied greatly causing the inputs and outputs to be inconsistent. As this is not

the case, it can safely be said that our model does not emulate the original box.
